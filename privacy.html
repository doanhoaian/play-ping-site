3. Network Simulation & VpnService Disclosure

This section is critical for understanding how the Application functions regarding Android’s VpnService API.

The Application utilizes the Android VpnService class to create a local, virtual network interface. This is the core mechanism allowing PlayPing to simulate network conditions (packet loss, high latency, disconnection) without requiring root access.

  • Local Processing: The majority of traffic interception and manipulation occurs locally on your device. The Application acts as a "loopback" to delay or drop packets based on your configuration.
  • No Content Inspection: The Application does not decrypt, read, store, or analyze the content (payload) of your network traffic (e.g., passwords, messages, emails).
  • Connectivity Maintenance: In limited scenarios, to prevent total connectivity failure during complex simulations, traffic may be routed through intermediary relay servers. These servers function strictly as data conduits; they do not log traffic content.
  • Not a VPN for Privacy: The Application is a developer tool for testing, not a consumer VPN designed for anonymity or bypassing geo-restrictions.
